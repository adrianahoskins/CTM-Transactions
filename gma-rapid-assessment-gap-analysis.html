80% Complete
Evidence — What the PPT Covers
  • WAEMU/BCEAO regulatory framework comprehensively documented (Slides 2–10) — Monetary union structure, banking system (160 institutions), 6 institution types, governance hierarchy, legislation hierarchy
  • Key legislation identified (Slide 8) — Loi Uniforme Bancaire (June 2023), Règlement 15/2002 (payment systems), Instruction 001-01-2024 (payment institutions), EMI Instruction 008-05-2015, AML/CFT law (2015/revised 2023), Règlement 06/2024 (new FX controls)
  • FX controls & capital restrictions (Slides 43–46) — Convertibility with restrictions (LCY→FCY requires documentation), no speculative hedging, FX derivatives now permitted for hedging under Reg. 06/2024. FCY account opening requires MoF + BCEAO approval.
  • 50% prepayment cap documented (Slide 22) — BCEAO Instruction 02.07.2025 RFE, Art. 6(f): advance payments cannot exceed 50% of goods/services value. Full compliance mandatory since Jan 2026. Maximum 6 months with 3-month grace period.
  • Repatriation methods with cost analysis (Slide 22) — 4 methods: Commodity prepayments (0.71%), FX capex prepayment (0.71%), Intercompany loan (2–3% friction), Dividend (15% WHT, permanent cost). Prioritization guidance included.
  • Depeg risk analysis (Slide 4) — Two scenarios analyzed: bilateral mutual agreement (requires WAMU unanimity + EU Council unanimity) and unilateral French withdrawal. Key monitoring indicators provided.
  • Trade transaction framework (Slides 45–46) — 3 import regimes (Freedom, Approval, Limitation). Grains = Category I (Freedom Arrangement). WAEMU CET: 5% + statistical duty 1% + AU levy 0.2% + community levies 1.3%.
  • Saisie (court-ordered freezes) (Slide 39) — Courts can order banks to freeze accounts/transactions due to vendor disputes. Happens at most banks except BOA.
  • Payment system architecture (Slide 9) — STAR-UEMOA (RTGS, ≥XOF 50M, real-time, cutoff 5:00 PM), SICA-UEMOA (domestic funds transfer,
  • BCEAO batch rejection risk (Slide 22) — If any payment in a submitted FX batch is incorrect, the entire batch gets rejected. When liquidity is tight, BCEAO finds more reasons to reject.

Evidence — What the PPT Covers

  • Wheat payment timing analyzed (Slides 23–25) — Average days with bank by bank and year for prepayments and remaining payments. Eurafrique receipt times (GMA debit to IOM credit) by bank by year. Source: GMA Daily Cash file.
  • Prefinancing arrangements documented (Slide 22 notes) — Some banks prefinance FX payments using their own reserves while BCEAO batch processes. SocGen fast-track program (2025). BOA more frequent. CITI started. SIB/Atlantique fast-track BCEAO approval internally.
  • Payroll process documented (Slide 40) — Currently paid through 4 banks (SIB, Atlantique, SocGen, BOA) to reduce fees and enable same-day for matching banks. Non-matching takes 1–2 days. PI-SPI consolidation opportunity identified.
  • Check & bill of exchange processes detailed (Slide 41) — Future-dated checks stored in safe, Joelle reviews daily and takes to bank on date. Bills of exchange endorsed same day, bank cashes on date. Affactourage product available but too expensive. Employee cash advance via check (≤XOF 200K). Bank check rejections for fees documented.
  • Cash depot operations (Slide 39) — 17+ depots with recommended cash limits. Cash deposited at nearest bank when limit reached or end of day. Frequency varies by depot volume. Cyril sending threshold.
  • Funds availability by payment type (Slide 53) — Full table: Wire/RTGS ≥XOF 50M (minutes–same day), Domestic Funds Transfer
  • 7-layer payment delay analysis (Slide 15) — Why payments take 2–3 days: Internal bank processing (0–2d), SICA batch cycle (+0–1d), receiving bank crediting (+0–1d), correspondent banking (+1–2d), weekends/holidays (+1–3d), AML/KYC holds (+1–5d), pre-PI-SPI gap (eliminated for members).
  • Dividend payment process (Slide 22) — Once per year. Board approval → General Assembly → documentation to bank → WHT payment → bank submits to BCEAO → payment sent to SOL. Must complete by Sept 30.
  • Intercompany loan process (Slide 22) — 6 steps: prepare agreement, board authorization, MoF declaration, registration fee, send funds, repay with interest. Transfer pricing concerns if outstanding too long.
  • Tax payment process (Slide 30) — BOA 7551 sub-account registered on tax authority platform. Keep balance near zero, fund only when payment due. Declaration → file → pay. Separate account mitigates risk of government accessing funds.
