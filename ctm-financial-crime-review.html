Executive Risk Assessment

This portfolio presents an elevated financial crime risk profile driven by five converging factors:

  1. A 30.6% transaction opacity rate (unclassified + "Other") representing ~$430M+ in combined value — above the ACAMS recommended maximum of 10–15%
  2. SWIFT/Telex transactions averaging $497K each with minimal narrative data, producing a $67.5M net outflow from 137 transactions
  3. A $951K net imbalance in inter-account transfers that cannot be explained by settlement timing over a 26-month window
  4. 24,952 cash transactions ($155.6M net cash inflow) in a GIABA-elevated risk corridor, with MTN Mobile Money as a structuring-risk channel
  5. Citibank Senegal: 461 txns, $140.8M debits, 60x portfolio average per-transaction value with $11.9M net outflow

While several patterns have plausible legitimate explanations consistent with West African banking norms, the combination warrants immediate escalation to the MLRO, SAR review on SWIFT/Telex activity, and GL reconciliation of the IAT imbalance.

F-02 IAT Net Imbalance — 576M XOF ($951K) Unexplained CRITICAL
IAT Transactions1,555 (669 matched pairs)
Debits$81.9M (49,571M XOF)
Credits$82.9M (50,147M XOF)
Net Surplus+$951K (credit-heavy)
Unmatched Entries217 transactions

Internal account transfers in a closed system must net to approximately zero over 26 months. The $951K credit surplus is not a rounding artifact. Three possible explanations:

  • Settlement timing (largely benign): WAEMU settlement can produce posting date mismatches of 1–2 business days. The matching algorithm uses exact same-date matching, which misses cross-day pairs.
  • Algorithm false positives: Coincidental same-date/same-amount matches between unrelated transactions create false pairs in a 144K-transaction dataset.
  • One-directional flows (requires investigation): Credit-heavy imbalance is consistent with "credit washing" — receiving funds from outside the monitored universe without a traceable offsetting debit.
Regulatory: BSA 31 CFR 1010.415 (Funds transfer recordkeeping), FATF R.10 (Ongoing CDD), CBUEMOA Instruction 01-2006 (IAT rules for UEMOA)

Recommended Actions

  • Full GL reconciliation of all inter-account movements across 15 banks for the 26-month period
  • Upgrade IAT matching: add ±1 business day tolerance and reference number matching
  • Identify all IAT credits with no matching debit — these are the investigation targets
  • If gap cannot be explained within 30 days, file SAR and notify internal audit

The same-date/same-amount matching algorithm presents false positive risk — coincidental matches reclassify genuine third-party payments as internal transfers, reducing AML scrutiny.

Recommended Actions
